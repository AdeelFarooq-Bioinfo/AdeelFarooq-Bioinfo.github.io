Teaching & Mentoring

Alongside my research in microbial genomics and bioinformatics, I have been actively involved in teaching and mentoring at the University of Guelph and beyond. My experience includes guiding graduate students, mentoring junior researchers, and delivering lectures in areas such as microbial genomics, plasmid biology, bioinformatics pipelines, and computational tools for metabolic reconstruction. I emphasize reproducible research practices, collaborative learning, and hands-on training to equip students with practical and analytical skills for modern microbiology and systems biology.

  • Mentorship: Supervised and trained graduate students in microbial genomics and antibiotic resistance research.
  • Lectures & Tutorials: Delivered sessions on genomic data analysis, visualization, and computational biology methods.
  • Workshops: Contributed to skill-based workshops on programming for life sciences, high-throughput sequencing data analysis, and network visualization.
  • Collaborative Supervision: Supported interdisciplinary projects by mentoring students from microbiology, computational sciences, and metabolic potential profiling.

Selected software
